1️⃣ **Personal Background**

**- Can you tell me about yourself?**
sum up → 요약하다
point out → 강조하다
upbringing → 성장 과정
personality → 성격
in a nutshell → 간단히 말하자면

_“In a nutshell, I’m a driven and detail-oriented person who thrives in fast-paced environments.”_

**- How would you describe yourself in three words?**
come up with → 생각해내다
stand out → 돋보이다
trait → 특성
reliability → 신뢰성
wear many hats → 여러 역할을 해내다

_“I’d describe myself as reliable, curious, and adaptable—I can wear many hats when needed.”_

**- What are your strengths?**
rely on → 의존하다
bring out → 이끌어내다
persistence → 끈기
initiative → 주도성
play to my strengths → 강점을 살리다

_“I always try to play to my strengths by staying organized and proactive in any project.”_

**- What are your weaknesses?**
work on → 개선하다
hold back → 억제하다
impatience → 조급함
tendency → 경향
a work in progress → 발전 중인 것

_“My perfectionism is still a work in progress—I’m learning to focus on impact, not just details.”_


2️⃣ **Skills & Experience**

**- Can you describe a project you’re proud of?**
carry out → 수행하다
pay off → 결실을 맺다
collaboration → 협력
outcome → 결과
take pride in → 자부심을 느끼다

_“I take pride in leading a cross-department project that increased efficiency by 30%.”_

**- How do you handle challenges or difficulties at work?**
cope with → 대처하다
push through → 끝까지 해내다
composure → 침착함
resourcefulness → 임기응변 능력
keep a cool head → 냉정을 유지하다

_“Even under pressure, I try to keep a cool head and focus on finding a practical solution.”_

**- How do you deal with a difficult coworker?**
talk over → 논의하다
sort out → 해결하다
conflict → 갈등
empathy → 공감력
agree to disagree → 의견 차이를 인정하다

_“When conflicts arise, I try to agree to disagree and focus on the shared goal.”_


3️⃣ **Goals & Motivation**

**- What are your short-term and long-term goals?**
aim for → 목표로 하다
build toward → 향해 나아가다
ambition → 야망
progression → 발전
set the bar high → 높은 목표를 세우다

_“I set the bar high by aiming to become a team leader within three years while developing leadership skills.”_

**- What motivates you to do your best at work?**
draw from → ~에서 얻다
push forward → 계속 나아가다
fulfillment → 성취감
challenge → 도전
drive me forward → 나를 앞으로 나아가게 하다

_“Solving complex problems drives me forward because it gives me a strong sense of fulfillment.”_

**- What kind of work environment helps you perform best?**
depend on → ~에 달려 있다
adapt to → 적응하다
autonomy → 자율성
atmosphere → 분위기
bring out the best in me → 나의 잠재력을 끌어내다

_“A collaborative environment brings out the best in me because I value open communication.”_

**- What does success mean to you?**
live up to → 기대에 부응하다
move toward → 향해 나아가다
satisfaction → 만족
integrity → 진정성
measure of success → 성공의 척도

_“For me, the true measure of success is growth that aligns with my personal values.”_

▪️ highlight your strengths
: 자신의 강점을 강조하다
“She highlighted her strengths during the interview.”
그녀는 면접에서 자신의 강점을 강조했습니다.

▪️ admit your weaknesses
: 자신의 약점을 솔직하게 인정하다
“It’s okay to admit your weaknesses if you show how you’re improving.”
개선하려는 노력을 함께 말한다면 약점을 인정해도 괜찮습니다.

💡 Tip #1 – “strength” vs. “advantage” vs. “skill”
strength → 개인적 강점 (성격, 태도)
advantage → 비교 우위 (다른 사람보다 나은 점)
skill → 기술적 능력 (배워서 익힌 능력)

▪️ follow up
: 후속 연락을 하다
“Don’t forget to follow up with a thank-you email.”
감사 이메일을 보내는 걸 잊지 마세요.

▪️ break the ice
: 어색한 분위기를 깨다
“The interviewer made a joke to break the ice.”
면접관이 농담을 해서 분위기를 풀었습니다.

💡 Tip #2 – “get + p.p.” 구조 자주 쓰이는 표현
get hired → 고용되다
get promoted → 승진하다
get rejected → 불합격되다
get fired → 해고되다

▪️ ask insightful questions
: 통찰력 있는 질문을 하다
“He asked insightful questions that impressed the interviewer.”
그는 면접관에게 깊은 인상을 주는 통찰력 있는 질문을 했습니다.

▪️ leave a strong impression
: 강한 인상을 남기다
“She left a strong impression with her enthusiasm.”
그녀는 열정으로 강한 인상을 남겼습니다.

💡 Tip #3 – “nonverbal” communication
eye contact → 눈 맞춤
posture → 자세
gesture → 손짓, 몸짓
facial expression → 표정
