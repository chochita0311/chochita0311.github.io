**1) Personal Gift Experiences**

**- What’s the best gift you’ve ever received?**
treasured → 소중히 여기는
memorable → 기억에 남는
hold onto → 간직하다
cherish → 아끼다, 소중히 하다
a gift to remember → 오래 기억할 선물

_"The watch my parents gave me for graduation is something I truly cherish and will hold onto forever."_

**- Have you ever received a gift you didn’t like but pretended to enjoy?**
insincere → 진심이 아닌
awkward → 어색한
put on a smile → 억지로 웃다
play along with → 맞장구치다, 맞춰주다, 동의하는 척하다
grin and bear it → 억지로 참다

_"I didn’t like the sweater at all, but I just put on a smile and decided to grin and bear it."_

**- What’s the most expensive gift you’ve ever received?**
luxurious → 고급스러운
extravagant → 사치스러운
splash out on → 큰돈을 쓰다
show off → 자랑하다
cost an arm and a leg → 엄청 비싸다

_"The diamond necklace must have cost an arm and a leg, but my friend never tried to show off about it."_

**- Has a gift ever made you emotional or cry?**
heartfelt → 진심 어린
sentimental → 감정적인
tear up → 눈물이 나다
open up → 마음을 열다
hit me right in the feels → 마음을 울리다

_"When I read the letter that came with the gift, I couldn’t help but tear up—it really hit me right in the feels."_

**- Have you ever received the same gift more than once?**
identical → 똑같은
repetitive → 반복적인
end up with → 결국 ~을 가지게 되다
give away → 주다, 양도하다
déjà vu → 데자뷔 같은 느낌

_"I’ve ended up with three identical mugs over the years—it was like déjà vu every birthday."_


**2) Gift Preferences & Opinions**

**- Do you prefer practical gifts or sentimental gifts?**
functional → 실용적인
emotional → 감정적인
lean toward → ~쪽으로 기울다
depend on → ~에 따라 다르다
hit close to home → 마음에 와닿다

_"I usually lean toward functional gifts, but sometimes a sentimental one can really hit close to home."_

**- Would you rather receive one big gift or many small ones?**
substantial → 상당한, 큰
varied → 다양한
break down → 나누다
spread out → 분산하다
quality over quantity → 양보다 질

_"I prefer one substantial gift—always quality over quantity for me."_

**- Do you like gift cards as presents?**
versatile → 다용도의
convenient → 편리한
stock up on → 비축하다
use up → 다 쓰다
the gift of choice → 선택의 자유를 주는 선물

_"Gift cards give me the gift of choice, so I can stock up on what I really want."_

**- Do you think giving cash is too impersonal?**
thoughtless → 성의 없는
straightforward → 직설적인, 단순한
hand over → 건네주다
miss out on → 놓치다
money talks → 돈이 모든 것을 가능하게 한다

_"Some say giving cash is thoughtless, but honestly, sometimes money talks."_

**- Would you be happy if someone gave you food as a gift?**
delicious → 맛있는
homemade → 집에서 만든
dig into → 맛있게 먹다
wrap up → 포장하다
the way to someone’s heart is through their stomach → 음식이 최고의 선물

_"If it’s homemade, I’ll gladly dig into it—after all, the way to someone’s heart is through their stomach."_


**3) Creative & Unusual Gifts**

**- What’s the most unique gift you’ve ever seen?**
one-of-a-kind → 유일한
memorable → 기억에 남는
stand out → 눈에 띄다
come across → 우연히 발견하다
like nothing else → 전혀 다른, 특별한

_"The handmade wooden music box really stood out—it was like nothing else I’ve ever seen."_

**- Have you ever received a funny or joke gift?**
hilarious → 아주 웃긴
silly → 유치한
burst out laughing → 폭소하다
play a prank on → 장난치다
pull someone’s leg → 놀리다

_"My friend once gave me a giant rubber chicken just to pull my leg, and I burst out laughing."_

**- Have you ever made a surprise gift for someone without a reason?**
spontaneous → 즉흥적인
thoughtful → 사려 깊은
whip up → 급히 만들다
hand out → 건네주다
out of the blue → 불쑥, 느닷없이

_"I once whipped up cookies and gave them to my neighbor out of the blue—she was so happy."_

**- If you could give yourself a gift right now, what would it be?**
deserve → ~할 자격이 있다
indulgent → 사치스러운, 즐기는
treat myself to → 나 자신에게 ~를 선물하다
save up for → ~를 위해 저축하다
splurge on → 큰돈을 쓰다

_"I would treat myself to a luxury spa day and just splurge on relaxation."_
