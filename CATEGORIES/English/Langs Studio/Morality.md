1️⃣ **Personal Beliefs & Choices**

**- Do you think people are naturally good or bad?**
nature → 본성
tendency → 성향
come down to → 결국 ~이 되다
grapple with → 씨름하다
a gray area → 애매한 영역

_" When I grapple with the question of whether people are naturally good or bad, I often feel it’s a gray area that comes down to context and upbringing."_

**- Have you ever had to make a difficult moral choice?**
conscience → 양심
dilemma → 딜레마
weigh up → 저울질하다
go through with → 끝까지 하다
torn between two sides → 양쪽 사이에서 갈등하다

_"I was once torn between two sides when I had to weigh up loyalty to a friend against telling the truth in a difficult situation."_

**- Do you believe lying can ever be “good”?**
deception → 속임수
intention → 의도
cover up → 숨기다
justify → 정당화하다
for the greater good → 더 큰 선을 위해

_"Sometimes small deceptions can be justified for the greater good, like covering up a detail to protect someone’s feelings."_

2️⃣ **White Lies & Honesty**

**- Do you think it’s okay to lie about liking a gift?**
gratitude → 감사
gesture → 행위, 표시
make up → 지어내다
hold back → 숨기다
sugarcoat the truth → 진실을 좋게 포장하다

_"When someone gives me a gift, I usually sugarcoat the truth because I value the gesture more than my personal preference."_

**- Is “I’m on my way” still a lie if you haven’t left the house yet?**
excuse → 변명
intention → 의도
stretch the truth → 진실을 과장하다
put off → 미루다
bend the truth → 진실을 약간 왜곡하다

_"Saying ‘I’m on my way’ before leaving is just bending the truth a little, since the intention is usually to put off conflict rather than deceive."_

**- Would you tell a friend the truth if their haircut looked bad?**
appearance → 외모
opinion → 의견
hold back → 숨기다
speak up → 솔직히 말하다
honesty is the best policy → 정직이 최선이다

_"Even though honesty is the best policy, I sometimes hold back when speaking up could unnecessarily hurt my friend’s confidence."_

3️⃣ **Modern Dilemmas**

**- Is it wrong to use ChatGPT or AI to do homework?**
plagiarism → 표절
shortcut → 지름길
rely on → 의존하다
hand in → 제출하다
cut corners → 편법을 쓰다

_"Some students cut corners by relying too heavily on AI to hand in assignments, but whether it’s truly wrong depends on how much they learn from the process."_

**- Do you think using ad-blockers is unfair to creators?**
revenue → 수익
support → 지원하다
cut off → 끊다
rely on → 의존하다
a double-edged sword → 양날의 검

_"Ad-blockers feel like a double-edged sword, because while they cut off creators’ revenue, they also protect users from overwhelming ads."_

**- Do you think online behavior should follow the same moral rules as real life?**
ethics → 윤리
standard → 기준
live up to → 따르다
carry over → 이어지다
what you see is what you get → 있는 그대로

_"I believe moral standards should carry over online, because what you see is what you get isn’t always true in the digital world."_

▪️moral judgment
: 도덕적 판단
“His moral judgment guided the team through the crisis.”
그의 도덕적 판단이 팀을 위기에서 이끌었습니다.

▪️ethical behavior
: 윤리적 행동
“Ethical behavior is essential in medicine and law.”
윤리적 행동은 의학과 법률 분야에서 필수적입니다.

💡 Tip #1 – “virtue” vs. “quality” vs. “trait”
"virtue" = 도덕적 미덕 (patience is a virtue)
"quality" = 일반적인 성질/특성 (leadership quality)
"trait" = 개인적 특성 (honest trait)

▪️ethical violation
: 윤리 위반
“The company was fined for ethical violations.”
그 회사는 윤리 위반으로 벌금을 부과받았습니다.

▪️conflict of interest
: 이해 충돌
“Politicians should avoid any conflict of interest.”
정치인들은 어떠한 이해 충돌도 피해야 합니다.

💡 Tip #2 – “violation” vs. “breach”
"violation" = 규칙, 법, 윤리 위반 (ethical violation, traffic violation)
"breach" = 계약, 약속 위반 (breach of contract, breach of trust)

▪️honorable action
: 명예로운 행동
“Returning the lost wallet was an honorable action.”
잃어버린 지갑을 돌려준 것은 명예로운 행동이었습니다.

▪️moral courage
: 도덕적 용기
“Whistleblowers need moral courage to speak the truth.”
내부 고발자는 진실을 말할 도덕적 용기가 필요합니다.

💡 Tip #3 – “courage” vs. “bravery”
"courage" = 두려움 속에서도 옳은 행동 (moral courage)
"bravery" = 위험에 맞서는 용기 (physical bravery)
