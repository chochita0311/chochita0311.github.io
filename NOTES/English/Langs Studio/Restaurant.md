1️⃣ **Eating Out Habits**

**- How often do you eat out?**
go out for → ~하러 나가다
cut down on → 줄이다
frequency → 빈도
moderation → 절제
every now and then → 가끔씩

_“I try to eat out only every now and then to stay healthy and save money.”_

**- What kind of restaurants do you usually go to?**
go for → 선택하다
try out → 시도하다
variety → 다양성
atmosphere → 분위기
hidden gem → 숨겨진 명소

_“I love checking out hidden gems that serve authentic local cuisine.”_

**- Do you care about a restaurant’s interior or just the food?**
pay attention to → 신경 쓰다
focus on → 집중하다
decor → 인테리어
impression → 인상
first impressions matter → 첫인상이 중요하다

_“I believe first impressions matter, so I tend to prefer places with warm ambiance.”_

**- How do you usually find new restaurants?**
come across → 우연히 발견하다
ask around → 물어보다
recommendation → 추천
platform → 플랫폼
word gets around → 소문이 퍼지다

_“Once a restaurant serves great food, word gets around quickly.”_

2️⃣ **Ordering & Dining Experience**

**- Do you prefer ordering the same food or trying something new?**
stick to → 고수하다
branch out → 새로운 걸 시도하다
curiosity → 호기심
comfort zone → 익숙한 영역
step out of your comfort zone → 익숙한 틀을 벗어나다

_“I try to step out of my comfort zone by ordering dishes I can’t even pronounce.”_

**- Have you ever complained about food at a restaurant?**
send back → 다시 돌려보내다
make up for → 보상하다
dissatisfaction → 불만
refund → 환불
the customer is always right → 손님이 항상 옳다

_“I rarely complain, but when the food is undercooked, I believe the customer is always right.”_

**- What do you usually do if a restaurant makes a mistake with your order?**
point out → 지적하다
make up for → 보상하다
misunderstanding → 오해
politeness → 공손함
handle it gracefully → 우아하게 대처하다

_“If there’s a mix-up, I try to handle it gracefully rather than making a scene.”_

**- Have you ever had a memorable dining experience?**
take in → 경험하다
end up → 결국 ~하게 되다
occasion → 특별한 순간
impression → 인상
once in a lifetime experience → 평생 한 번뿐인 경험

_“Dining on a rooftop in Paris was truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience.”_

3️⃣ **Memorable Food Experiences**

**- Have you ever regretted trying a particular dish?**
throw up → 토하다
swear off → 다시는 하지 않기로 하다
regret → 후회
discomfort → 불쾌함
live and learn→ 경험을 통해 배우다

_“I once tried blue cheese for the first time—let’s just say I lived and learned.”_

**- What’s the most expensive meal you’ve ever paid for?**
pay for → 지불하다
splurge on → 과하게 쓰다
luxury → 사치
experience → 경험
worth every penny → 한 푼도 아깝지 않은

_“That Michelin dinner was worth every penny—it was pure culinary art.”_

**- Have you ever had a meal that changed your opinion about food?**
open up to → 마음을 열다
learn from → 배우다
perception → 인식
transformation → 변화
eye-opening → 새로운 시각을 열어주는

_“Trying vegan cuisine for the first time was an eye-opening experience that changed my view on food.”_

**Work out**
운동하다
I try to work out three times a week to stay healthy.

**Eat out**
외식하다
We decided to eat out since no one wanted to cook tonight.

**Cardios**
유산소 운동
Doing cardios regularly helps improve heart health.

**Room temperature**
실온
Store the medicine at room temperature.

**Street food**
길거리 음식
Street food is often cheap and full of local flavor.

**Polarizing**
논란을 일으키는, 의견이 갈리는
The movie received polarizing reviews from critics.

**Fancy**
고급의, 화려한; ~하고 싶다
They went to a fancy restaurant for their anniversary dinner.

**Affordable**
가격이 알맞은, 부담 없는
The brand’s goal is to offer stylish yet affordable clothing.

**Play it safe**
안전하게 행동하다, 위험을 피하다
He decided to play it safe and invest in low-risk stocks.

**Search engine**
검색 엔진
Google is the most widely used search engine in the world.

**Ratings**
평가, 시청률
The show’s ratings increased after the new season premiered.

**Cafeteria**
구내식당, 카페테리아
The company cafeteria serves both Korean and Western food.

**Picky eater**
편식하는 사람
My little brother is such a picky eater — he only eats noodles.

**Can’t be bothered**
귀찮아서 ~하지 않다
I can’t be bothered to cook tonight, let’s just order delivery.

**Takes too long**
너무 오래 걸리다
I stopped going there because the service takes too long.

**Conglomerate**
대기업, 복합기업
Samsung is one of the largest conglomerates in South Korea.

**Business card**
명함
He handed me his business card after the meeting.

**Accommodation**
숙소, 숙박시설
We found affordable accommodation near the beach.
