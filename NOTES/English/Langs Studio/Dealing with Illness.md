1️⃣ **Personal Experiences**

**- How do you usually deal with being sick — rest, medicine, or natural remedies?**
symptom → 증상
remedy → 치료법
come down with → ~에 걸리다
fight off → 이겨내다
under the weather → 몸이 안 좋은

_“When I’m feeling under the weather, I usually rest and drink tea instead of taking heavy medicine.”_

**- Do you go to the doctor easily, or only when it feels serious?**
appointment → 진료 예약
diagnosis → 진단
check up on → ~을 검사하다
put off → 미루다
better safe than sorry → 조심해서 나쁠 건 없다

_“I try not to put off doctor visits — it’s better safe than sorry when it comes to health.”_

**- What’s the most memorable time you were sick?**
fever → 열
recovery → 회복
get over → 회복하다
throw up → 토하다
knock me out → 기진맥진하게 하다

_“That stomach flu really knocked me out — I couldn’t eat anything for three days.”_

2️⃣ **Habits & Prevention**

**- Do you take vitamins or supplements regularly?**
supplement → 보충제
immune system → 면역 체계
stock up on → ~을 사두다
stick to → 꾸준히 하다
an apple a day keeps the doctor away → 사과 하나면 의사가 필요 없다

_“I try to stick to my vitamin routine — after all, an apple a day keeps the doctor away.”_

**- How do you try to stay healthy and avoid getting sick?**
hygiene → 위생
routine → 일상 습관
work out → 운동하다
cut down on → ~을 줄이다
prevention is better than cure → 예방이 치료보다 낫다

_“I work out regularly and cut down on junk food because prevention is better than cure.”_

**- Do you believe in traditional remedies or prefer modern medicine?**
herbal medicine → 한약, 약초 치료
prescription → 처방전
rely on → ~에 의지하다
turn to → ~에 기대다
old but gold → 옛것이지만 여전히 좋은

_“I sometimes turn to herbal medicine when I catch a cold — those old but gold remedies still work.”_

3️⃣ **Social & Cultural Perspectives**

**- Do you think it’s okay to go to work or school with a mild cold?**
contagious → 전염성의
sick leave → 병가
call in sick → 아프다고 연락하다
spread around → 퍼뜨리다
better to be safe than sorry → 조심해서 나쁠 건 없다

_“Even with a mild cold, I call in sick — it’s better to be safe than sorry and not spread it around.”_

**- Do you think people fake sickness sometimes to rest or escape responsibilities?**
excuse → 핑계
burnout → 탈진
make up → 지어내다
get away with → 교묘히 피하다
pull a sickie → 꾀병 부리다

_“Some people pull a sickie once in a while, especially when they’re burnt out from work.”_

**- How has COVID-19 changed how people think about illness?**
pandemic → 전염병 대유행
awareness → 인식
open up to → ~에 마음을 열다
adapt to → ~에 적응하다
hit close to home → 실감나게 다가오다

_“The pandemic really hit close to home — it made everyone more aware of hygiene and self-care.”_

▪️ home remedy
: 민간요법, 가정 요법
“My grandma’s home remedy for colds is honey tea.”
우리 할머니의 감기 민간요법은 꿀차예요.

▪️ over-the-counter medicine
: 일반의약품 (처방전 없이 살 수 있는 약)
“You can buy this cream as an over-the-counter medicine.”
이 크림은 일반의약품으로 살 수 있습니다.

💡 Tip #1 – ‘remedy’는 약뿐 아니라 ‘해결책’ 의미로도 자주 사용됨!
예: There’s no quick remedy for stress. (스트레스에는 빠른 해결책이 없다.)

▪️ catch up on rest
: 충분히 쉬다, 휴식을 보충하다
“After being sick, I just need to catch up on rest.”
아프고 나서 휴식을 좀 보충해야 해요.

▪️ recover from (an illness)
: 병에서 회복하다
“It took me a week to recover from the flu.”
독감에서 회복하는 데 일주일 걸렸어요.

💡 Tip #2 – ‘heal’ vs. ‘recover’
'heal' → 상처나 부상이 낫다 (My cut is healing fast.)
'recover' → 병이나 피로에서 회복하다 (She’s recovering from surgery.)

▪️ take sick leave
: 병가를 내다
“He had to take sick leave for a week.”
그는 일주일 동안 병가를 내야 했습니다.

▪️ feel under the weather
: 몸이 좀 안 좋은, 컨디션이 나쁜 (비격식 표현)
“I’m feeling a bit under the weather today.”
오늘은 몸이 좀 안 좋네요.

💡 Tip #3 – 일상회화에서 자주 쓰이는 표현!
“under the weather”는 “감기 걸린 것 같다”처럼 가벼운 몸살 상태를 의미합니다.
