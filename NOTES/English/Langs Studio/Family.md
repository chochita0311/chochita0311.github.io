1️⃣ **Family Background & Childhood**

**-Did you resemble your mom or dad more when you were a child?**
take after → 닮다
pass down → 물려주다
genes → 유전자
trait → 특징
chip off the old block → 부모와 판박이

_“Many people said I was a chip off the old block, taking after my father’s traits and appearance.”_

**-How many siblings do you have, and what was your relationship like?**
get along with → 잘 지내다
fall out with → 다투다
rivalry → 경쟁
closeness → 친밀함
blood is thicker than water→ 피는 물보다 진하다

_“Even though my siblings and I sometimes fell out, we always patched things up because blood is thicker than water.”_

**-What values did your family emphasize the most?**
stick to → 고수하다
lay down → 정하다
honesty → 정직
perseverance → 인내심
stand one’s ground→ 자기 입장을 고수하다

_“My parents laid down strict rules, which taught me to stand my ground in difficult situations.”_

**-Did your parents have different parenting styles?**
clash with → 충돌하다
go against → 거스르다
strictness → 엄격함
leniency → 관대함
good cop, bad cop → 한쪽은 엄격, 한쪽은 관대

_“Their parenting often seemed like a good cop, bad cop approach, but eventually they struck a balance.”_

2️⃣ **Daily Family Life**

**-Did your family often eat together?**
sit down to → ~을 함께 시작하다
finish off → 다 먹다
mealtime → 식사 시간
gathering → 모임
break bread together → 함께 식사하다

“Every mealtime was an opportunity to break bread together and strengthen our family _bond.”_

**-How did your family spend weekends?**
hang out → 어울리다
kick back → 쉬다
leisure → 여가
outing → 외출
quality time → 의미 있는 시간

_“Spending weekends outdoors allowed us to hang out and enjoy quality time together.”_

**-Who usually cooked at home, and what was their signature dish?**
whip up → 뚝딱 만들다
try out → 시험해 보다
ingredient → 재료
flavor → 맛
secret ingredient → 비밀 재료

_“My mother often whipped up meals with a secret ingredient, which made her dishes unforgettable.”_

**-How did your family celebrate birthdays?**
look forward to → 기대하다
set up → 준비하다
celebration → 축하
gathering → 모임
the life of the party→ 파티의 중심 인물

_“My sister always looked forward to birthdays because she loved being the life of the party.”_

3️⃣ **Extended Family & Relatives**

**-How often do you visit your relatives?**
drop by → 잠깐 들르다
keep in touch → 연락을 유지하다
relative → 친척
frequency → 빈도
long time no see → 오랜만이야

_“Whenever I dropped by my relatives’ house, it always felt like a long time no see moment.”_

**-What role did uncles or aunts play in your life?**
look up to → 존경하다
rely on → 의지하다
guidance → 지도
affection → 애정
like a second parent→ 제2의 부모 같은

_“I always looked up to my uncle because he acted like a second parent in my life.”_

**-Did you grow up close to your cousins?**
hang around with → 어울리다
fall out with → 사이가 틀어지다
companionship → 동료애
closeness → 친밀함
partners in crime → 장난꾸러기 동지

_“My cousins and I were like partners in crime, always hanging around together during childhood.”_

**-Did you have a favorite uncle, aunt, or cousin?**
look after → 돌보다
stick with → 함께하다
affection → 애정
bond → 유대감
near and dear to my heart → 내 마음에 가까운, 소중한

_“One cousin was especially near and dear to my heart because she always cheered me up.”_

▪️family tree
: 가계도, 족보
“He made a family tree for his history project.”
그는 역사 과제를 위해 가계도를 만들었습니다.

▪️distant relative
: 먼 친척
“I met a distant relative at the wedding.”
저는 결혼식에서 먼 친척을 만났습니다.

💡 Tip #1 – "relative" vs. "relation"
"relative" = 친척 (사람)
"relation" = 관계 (개념)
예) She is my relative.
예) I have a good relation with my uncle.

▪️in-laws
: 배우자의 가족 (시댁, 처가)
“My in-laws are visiting this weekend.”
제 시댁/처가 식구들이 이번 주말에 방문합니다.

▪️family bond
: 가족 간의 유대
“Strong family bonds help children feel secure.”
강한 가족 유대는 아이들이 안정감을 느끼도록 돕습니다.

💡 Tip #2 – "chore" vs. "housework"
"chore" = 하기 싫은 집안일 (빨래, 청소 등)
"housework" = 집안일 일반적 표현

▪️breadwinner
: 가장 (경제적으로 가족을 책임지는 사람)
“She became the family’s breadwinner after graduation.”
그녀는 졸업 후 가족의 가장이 되었습니다.

▪️household chores
: 집안일
“Everyone in the family helps with household chores.”
가족 모두가 집안일을 돕습니다.

💡 Tip #3 – "household" vs. "home"
"household" = 한 집에 사는 사람들 (경제·관리적 의미)
"home" = 집, 가정 (감정적·일상적 의미)
